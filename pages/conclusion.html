Sandro Botticelli in the ArCo Knowledge Graph

Conclusion

This project examined how Sandro Botticelli is represented in the ArCo Knowledge Graph, using the painting HistoricOrArtisticProperty/1201008107 as the anchor resource.

SPARQL queries showed that catalogue and photographic metadata are well represented, while philosophical and interpretative context — though present in labels of related resources — is not always modelled through structured properties such as a-cd:hasSubject. Queries 4 and 6 provided graph-based evidence for this gap.

LLM comparison (zero-shot, few-shot and chain-of-thought prompting) supported the same conclusion from an art historical perspective. The hybrid approach — SPARQL first, then LLM, then scholarly verification — proved more reliable than either method alone.

The proposed enrichment links the painting to Neoplatonism as an a-cd:Subject and optionally to Marsilio Ficino as a core:Agent, reusing ArCo vocabulary rather than inventing new predicates. Future validation queries illustrate how the enriched graph would support direct semantic retrieval.

In sum, the project shows that combining knowledge graph exploration, ontology-aware enrichment and LLM-assisted analysis can make implicit cultural knowledge more explicit, searchable and reusable for heritage research.
