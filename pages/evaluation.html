Evaluation

11. Comparative Evaluation of Large Language Models

After the semantic gaps had been objectively identified through SPARQL exploration, Large Language Models were employed to retrieve contextual knowledge absent from the ArCo Knowledge Graph. Three prompting strategies were applied throughout the project: Standard Prompting, Few-shot Prompting and Chain-of-Thought Prompting. ChatGPT and Gemini were systematically compared, while the project structure also allowed comparison with Claude and Mistral. The objective was not to determine which model produced the longest answer, but which generated the most reliable semantic concepts suitable for knowledge graph enrichment.

15. Contribution to Knowledge Engineering

The project demonstrates an evidence-based methodology for enriching a cultural heritage knowledge graph. SPARQL queries were used to identify what was already present, preventing unnecessary duplication. LLMs acted as semantic assistants rather than authorities, producing candidate knowledge that was validated and formalised as RDF. This combination of symbolic knowledge representation and generative AI illustrates how knowledge engineering can bridge structured metadata and rich contextual interpretation.
