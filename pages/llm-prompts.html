LLM Knowledge Extraction and Validation

3.1 Objective

This stage addresses the semantic gaps identified during the SPARQL exploration. The objective is to use multiple Large Language Models (LLMs) to generate contextual information that is absent from the ArCo Knowledge Graph and evaluate whether this information can be transformed into RDF triples.

Describe Sandro Botticelli's 'Madonna con Bambino, San Giovannino e angeli'. Focus on:

  4. Renaissance cultural context.

Return factual information only.

3.8 Transition to RDF Enrichment

The validated statements produced by the LLMs will be converted into RDF triples in the next stage. Only information supported by reliable sources will be proposed for enriching the ArCo Knowledge Graph. See RDF Enrichment.

PROMPT 1 — Standard Prompt

You are an art historian specialising in Italian Renaissance painting.

Analyse Sandro Botticelli's painting "Madonna con Bambino, San Giovannino e angeli".

Provide a structured analysis including:

  1. The represented figures.
  2. The iconography.
  3. The symbolic meaning of the lilies.
  4. The theological meaning of the composition.
  5. The historical context of the painting.
  6. The relationship of the artwork to Renaissance humanism.
  7. Information that could enrich a cultural heritage knowledge graph.

Do not invent facts. Use only well-established art historical knowledge.

ChatGPT

Gemini

The Standard Prompt generated comprehensive responses in both ChatGPT and Gemini. Both models correctly identified the represented figures, including the Virgin Mary, the Christ Child, Saint John the Baptist and the accompanying angels. They also recognised the artwork as a Renaissance representation of the Sacra Conversazione and explained the principal iconographic characteristics of the composition.

In addition, both models provided information that is not explicitly represented in the ArCo Knowledge Graph, including the symbolic meaning of lilies, the theological interpretation of the Incarnation and the relationship between the painting and Renaissance Humanism. These semantic descriptions extend beyond the cataloguing metadata currently available in the ArCo Knowledge Graph and therefore represent valuable candidates for knowledge graph enrichment.

PROMPT 2 — Few-shot Prompt

You are an expert in cultural heritage knowledge graphs.

Example
Artwork: The Birth of Venus
Output
Represented figures: Venus, Zephyrus, Aura, Hora.
Iconography: The birth of Venus from classical mythology.
Symbolism: Beauty, purity, divine love.
Historical context: Florentine Renaissance.
Knowledge Graph enrichment: represented figures, iconography, symbolism, historical context

Now analyse Madonna con Bambino, San Giovannino e angeli. Return your answer using exactly the same structure.

The Few-shot Prompt produced more structured responses than the Standard Prompt. Because both models received an example before analysing Botticelli's painting, their answers followed a consistent semantic organisation. The information was divided into represented figures, iconography, symbolism, historical context and potential knowledge graph enrichment.

This structure facilitates comparison between different LLMs and simplifies the transformation of textual descriptions into RDF triples. From a knowledge engineering perspective, Few-shot prompting therefore improves the consistency of semantic extraction.

PROMPT 3 — Chain-of-Thought

You are an art historian. Think step by step.

Step 1. Identify every represented figure.
Step 2. Explain the iconography.
Step 3. Explain the symbolic meaning of each important object.
Step 4. Explain the theological interpretation.
Step 5. Explain the Renaissance cultural context.
Step 6. Identify which information is already present in the ArCo Knowledge Graph.
Step 7. Identify which information is missing from the ArCo Knowledge Graph.
Step 8. Suggest semantic information that could enrich the knowledge graph.

Return the final answer in structured sections.

ChatGPT

Gemini

The Chain-of-Thought Prompt generated the most detailed responses among all prompting strategies. Instead of immediately producing an answer, both models decomposed the task into several reasoning steps. This resulted in a clearer distinction between factual metadata already contained in the ArCo Knowledge Graph and additional semantic knowledge absent from the knowledge graph.

Both ChatGPT and Gemini explicitly identified which information was already available in the ArCo Knowledge Graph and proposed additional semantic concepts such as iconographic themes, symbolic meanings, theological interpretations and historical context. This reasoning- oriented approach produced the most useful information for RDF enrichment.

Comparative Analysis

The comparative evaluation demonstrates that both ChatGPT and Gemini generated high- quality semantic descriptions that substantially extend the information currently available in the ArCo Knowledge Graph. ChatGPT produced richer theological and symbolic interpretations, whereas Gemini generated responses that were more structured from a knowledge engineering perspective and more directly applicable to RDF modelling. Consequently, both systems proved highly effective for identifying semantic information suitable for cultural heritage knowledge graph enrichment.

Knowledge Extraction Summary

The comparison clearly indicates that the contribution of Large Language Models lies primarily at the semantic level rather than at the descriptive level. The ArCo Knowledge Graph already provides reliable cataloguing metadata, including authorship, dating, dimensions, materials, and basic subject information. In contrast, both ChatGPT and Gemini generated contextual knowledge concerning iconography, symbolism, theology, Renaissance cultural context, and semantic relationships between entities. These concepts correspond directly to the knowledge gaps identified during the SPARQL exploration and therefore represent the most promising candidates for RDF-based knowledge graph enrichment in the following stage of the project.
