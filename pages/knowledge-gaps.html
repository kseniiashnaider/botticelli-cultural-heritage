6. Evidence-Based Identification of Knowledge Gaps

The following SPARQL queries investigated whether higher-level semantic information was explicitly represented. The results showed that several important interpretative concepts were missing from the current RDF description. These missing concepts became the focus of the enrichment process.

6.1 Missing Iconclass Classification

Query 4 attempted to retrieve an Iconclass code but returned no value. Although the painting is well described, it is not linked to a controlled iconographic vocabulary. This prevents semantic comparison with artworks sharing the same iconographic tradition. ChatGPT and Gemini independently identified the painting as belonging to the Sacra Conversazione type, providing a validated candidate for enrichment.

6.2 Missing Symbolic Interpretation

Queries exploring contextual metadata did not reveal structured descriptions of symbolic meaning. The painting contains Marian symbolism, particularly through lilies, angels and the relationship between the Virgin and Child. Both language models consistently explained these symbols and associated them with purity, virginity and Marian devotion. These concepts were proposed as RDF semantic relationships.

6.3 Missing Theological Interpretation

The RDF graph contains factual descriptions but not theological interpretation. The LLM analysis identified concepts including the Incarnation, Divine Motherhood and the foreshadowing of Christ's Passion. These ideas enrich the semantic understanding of the artwork without modifying existing metadata.

6.4 Missing Renaissance Context

The graph contains historical metadata but does not explicitly connect the painting with Florentine Renaissance culture, Renaissance Humanism or Botticelli's intellectual environment. The LLM comparison consistently identified these concepts as central contextual information suitable for semantic enrichment.

7. Validation Using Large Language Models

Knowledge generation was performed using Standard Prompting, Few-shot Prompting and Chain-of-Thought Prompting. ChatGPT and Gemini were compared throughout the project. Candidate concepts were accepted only when they were consistent across models and supported by established art-historical knowledge. This validation reduced the risk of introducing unsupported semantic statements into the enrichment model. See LLM Prompts.

10. Conclusions

The project demonstrates a complete Knowledge Engineering workflow. Knowledge gaps were not assumed but objectively identified through SPARQL exploration. Large Language Models were subsequently employed as knowledge extraction tools rather than authoritative sources. Their outputs were compared, validated and transformed into RDF triples that enrich the cultural heritage description of Botticelli's artwork. The resulting methodology is transparent, reproducible and transferable to other cultural heritage objects. Most importantly, the project shows that semantic enrichment should be evidence- based: existing metadata must first be explored, genuine knowledge gaps identified, generated knowledge validated and only then incorporated into a knowledge graph.
