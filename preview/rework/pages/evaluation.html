Sandro Botticelli in the ArCo Knowledge Graph

Evaluation and Discussion

Evaluation of the knowledge graph (ArCo)

ArCo provides solid catalogue metadata for Botticelli's works: types, labels, locations, authorship and links to photographic reproductions. SPARQL exploration confirms that the graph is rich at the documentation level. Interpretative and philosophical context, however, is often implicit in labels of related resources rather than expressed through Context Description properties such as a-cd:hasSubject.

Comparison of approaches

Approach Strengths Limitations
KG-only (SPARQL) Verifiable, reproducible, grounded in actual triples; UNION query exposes implicit vs explicit information Cannot infer missing knowledge; REGEX on labels is a workaround, not a semantic relation
LLM-only Suggests art historical context (Neoplatonism, Medici, symbolism) quickly; useful for brainstorming enrichment Risk of unsupported claims; no guarantee that suggestions match ArCo modelling patterns
Hybrid (SPARQL + LLM) SPARQL proves the gap; LLM proposes candidates; scholar checks + ArCo properties shape the final enrichment More steps; requires aligning LLM output with ontology constraints

Evaluation of LLM-assisted analysis

Three models were compared under zero-shot, few-shot and chain-of-thought prompting (see LLM Prompts). Recurring themes — Neoplatonism, Humanism, Medici patronage — converged despite different phrasing. Few-shot prompts produced output closer to valid RDF shapes. Chain-of-thought helped justify the final enrichment choice. All LLM suggestions were treated as hypotheses until checked against SPARQL and sources such as the Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy.

Evaluation of the proposed enrichment

Linking the painting to Neoplatonism via a-cd:hasSubject addresses a documented gap and uses existing ArCo vocabulary. The validation queries in Validation show how the enriched graph would support direct retrieval of philosophical context.

Limitations

  • Enrichment was not deployed to the production ArCo endpoint
  • Neoplatonic influence on Botticelli is debated in art history — the triple represents a proposed interpretative layer, not catalogue fact
  • Query 4–6 results depend on how many photographic resources are linked to the anchor URI

Future work

  • Extend enrichment to Primavera and Birth of Venus with separate anchor resources
  • Add further subjects (Renaissance Humanism, mythological allegory)
  • Test RAG-style prompts with larger SPARQL subgraphs injected into LLM context
